## Full profile (~300 words)

Sander Boer is a developer and designer based in Amsterdam with 19+ years of hands-on software experience.

His path into programming started the way the best ones do — obsessively. While others played online games, Sander reverse-engineered one, hosting his own server, designing its database, and shipping a fully working bomb squad mod that players actually played. The tools changed. The instinct never did.

He studied Communication and Multimedia Design, then went further with a master's in Digital Design — not to add credentials, but because the intersection of craft and code is where he does his best work. Most teams split design and engineering into separate silos. Sander sits in the middle, fluent in both, and uses that to close the gap between good engineering and great experience.

His projects reflect the range. **Microflow** gives designers a node-based editor to prototype microcontroller interactions without touching firmware. **Fissa** lets party guests vote on what plays next, built on React Native, tRPC, and Spotify. **Assessor Bot** uses LLMs to give students instant, honest feedback without waiting for a teacher. **VeileNext** dragged the world's largest flower auction into the modern web. The scale and domain vary — the quality bar doesn't.

Outside the screen, Sander gives workshops, mentors students, and experiments with 3D printing, microcontrollers, and AR/VR. He believes teaching is the fastest way to actually understand something, not just use it.

He is currently open to new work and collaborations.
